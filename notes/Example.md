## Introduction to Nursing Practice

### The Importance of the Nursing Role

*   Nurses are key players in society and the health sector.
*   The ability to know and understand your role helps you stand out and play your part effectively.
*   **Expectation for Students:** Take the learning process seriously and personally. Success requires dedication and intentionality in participation.

## Defining Nursing, Health, and Illness

### Definition of Nursing

*   **American Nurses Association (ANA, 1995) Definition:** Nursing is defined as the diagnosis and treatment of human response to health and illness.
*   **Nurse's Responsibility:** The primary duty of a nurse is to diagnose and treat human responses to health and illness.

### Definition of Health

*   **World Health Organization (WHO) Definition:** Health is defined as a state of complete physical, social, and psychological well-being of an individual, and not merely the absence of disease or deformity.

### Definition of Illness

*   Illness is defined as a deviation from health.

## Qualities of a Good Nurse

### Core Attributes

1.  **Compassion**
    *   Showing genuine concern and empathy for the patient's physical and emotional needs.
    *   Nurses must maintain compassion despite the volume of work or demanding tasks.

2.  **Communication Skills**
    *   Nurses must communicate effectively with the patient, the patient's family, and the entire healthcare team.
    *   Communication with patients and families should use simple, understandable terms.
    *   Effective communication is required with team members, including those above and below the nurse.

3.  **Critical Thinking**
    *   The ability to think critically and make sound decisions.
    *   Critical thinking is dependent on being theoretically oriented (knowing the rationale behind actions).
    *   Nurses must be equipped with knowledge to make strong decisions in the profession.

4.  **Attention to Details**
    *   Accurately administering medication (e.g., avoiding errors like 0.01 vs. 0.1).
    *   Accurately recording data.
    *   Monitoring patient vital signs accurately.
    *   Documenting all procedures appropriately.

5.  **Patience**
    *   Patience is a virtue necessary for dealing with people and their situations.
    *   Requires the ability to listen to people.
    *   Nurses should not always be in a hurry.
    *   Be patient with patients, colleagues, and the healthcare team.

6.  **Professionalism**
    *   Maintaining ethical standards in all activities.
    *   Respecting confidentiality and demonstrating integrity.
    *   Maintaining the professional image of a nurse (cleanliness, neatness, respectfulness).
    *   Avoiding gossiping, inappropriate dressing, or improper speech.

7.  **Adaptability (Flexibility)**
    *   The ability to adapt to any situation.
    *   Adapting to changes, new procedures, new environments, and new cases encountered in the profession.

8.  **Teamwork**
    *   No individual is an island; nurses must work with others.
    *   Working effectively with doctors, colleagues, and other healthcare providers.
    *   Teamwork promotes the safety and recovery of the patient.

9.  **Physical and Emotional Stamina**
    *   Displaying physical and emotional maturity and resilience.
    *   The ability to withstand demanding situations.
    *   Developing "thick skin" to tolerate challenges and pressure from school, home, or the career.
    *   Maintaining determination and the spirit to achieve goals.

10. **Dedication to Lifelong Learning**
    *   Learning has no ending in the profession.
    *   Staying updated with current trends, practices, and information.
    *   Maintaining an open mind to learning.
